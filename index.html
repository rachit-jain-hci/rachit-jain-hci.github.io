About me

I'm a final year student at IIIT-Delhi who wants to make lives comfortable. In college, I came across the field of "Human Computer Interaction", and which led me to read Norman's "Design of Everyday Things". As I dived deeper into HCI, Research and Design, I realised that this what what I want to do ahead- making experiences and interactions comfy.

I picked up projects in human-centered design, ethnography and AI, alongwith my computer science education. Learning that I enjoyed wearing hats of a researcher, designer and leader, I started to explore different interfaces like AR and VR as well. What exactly do I enjoy?

I enjoy the journey of exploration. I like to tackle complex problems, trying to make sense of them and navigating ambiguity. I enjoy working with people to synthesize this information into actionable ideas. And I love to advocate for a comfy, human-friendly technological solutions.

Currently, I am working towards helping people learn a language in mixed reality, researching how the GenZ in Delhi uses and buys sneakers, rekindling my love for dancing, and actively mentoring 60+ undergraduate design students build some amazing interactive systems.
